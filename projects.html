Projects (Graduate Student)

Award-Winning Political Science Research

Presented original research at the April 2024 Fulton Conference and received 3rd place in the Political Science division, competing against 97 entries. The project showcased deep analytical insight on legislative behavior and political outcomes, and was recognized for clarity, innovation, and rigor.

Federal Policy Research – The McKeon Group

During my time with The McKeon Group, I conducted research on legislative and administrative policy, tracked political trends, and produced over 50 research memorandums. I collaborated with congressional staff to help draft impactful legislation and prepared strategic briefs for clients navigating complex federal policy environments.

Utah State Legislature Internship

As a legislative intern for the Senate Rules Chair, I facilitated communication between legislators and stakeholders, managed workflow in a fast-paced committee environment, and reviewed over 150 bills. This experience sharpened my understanding of local governance and legislative strategy in practice.

Career Path Preview – Legislative Performance Auditor

Beginning in April 2025, I will start a new role as a Legislative Performance Auditor with the Office of the Legislative Auditor General (OLAG) in Utah. OLAG is a nonpartisan office that supports the Utah Legislature by conducting independent audits to evaluate the efficiency, effectiveness, and accountability of state programs and agencies. This role allows me to stay close to state politics without being directly involved in it, offering a meaningful opportunity to contribute to the policymaking process through investigative analysis.

I look forward to applying my skills in quantitative analysis, writing, and communication to identify areas for improvement in public service delivery. While I don’t yet know what specific projects I’ll be working on, I’m excited by the prospect of digging into complex and impactful topics using a mix of tools—from data analysis in Excel and R to in-depth research and interviews. This role is a natural next step in my commitment to evidence-based public administration.
